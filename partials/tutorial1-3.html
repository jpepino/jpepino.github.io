Time Signature, Beats, and Measure

Previously, we mentioned the concept of duration. While we will cover the more complex notions of duration in a later lesson, it is important to understand the basics now. A beat is the basic unit of time. One can think of a beat as a single tap of the foot when listening along to a song. Beats are fundamental concepts in music, a note can last one beat, multiple beats, or a fraction of a beat.

A bar separates a sequence of notes based on their duration. The beats contained between each bar is called the measure. The time signature defines how many beats are in each measure, and similarly how many beats are between each bar. For example, in the 4/4 time signature pictured above, there are 4 beats in a measure. In a 3/4 time signature, there are 3 beats in a measure. We will disregard the bottom number for now.
