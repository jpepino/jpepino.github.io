Reading Notes

Now that we understand the basic terminology of music, let's begin placing notes on the staff and learning how to read them. To recall, each line or space between the lines is a different pitch, which is represented by a musical note. Alphabetically, these notes are A, B, C, D, E, F, and G. After reaching G it repeats back to A, but the A is now one octave higher than the original A you started on.

The daunting task of learning how to read music is memorizing which line or space corresponds with each note. Looking above and starting from the lower notes and working our way up, we can see that the notes which are situated on the lines are E, G, B, D and F. The notes situated in the spaces are F, A, C and E. A useful mantra for remembering the notes on the line is remembering a phrase, such as Every Good Boy Deserves Fudge. A useful way of remembering the notes in the spaces is simply that it spells out the word "face".
