The Treble Clef

A clef, in music, is a symbol which indicates the pitch of the notes that are written after it. In the majority of modern music notation, the two clefs which are used are the treble clef (pictured on the staff above) and the bass clef, which will be covered in an advanced lesson.

Pianists will have musical sheets which will have these two clefs joined together, with the right hand playing the treble clef, and the left hand playing the bass clef. Instruments which generally play notes of a higher pitch will only have the treble clef, such as the flute and clarinet. Instruments whose music consists of only the bass clef include the trombone, or the cello.
